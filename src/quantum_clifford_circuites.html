Introduction

Quantum Clifford circuits are a subset of quantum circuits that can be efficiently simulated classically. They play a crucial role in quantum computing and quantum error correction.

Clifford Gates

Clifford gates are a set of quantum gates that include the Hadamard gate, the Phase gate, and the CNOT gate. These gates form the Clifford group, which is closed under composition and inversion.

Stabilizer States

Stabilizer states are quantum states that can be described using stabilizer formalism. They are the states that can be generated by applying Clifford gates to the computational basis states.

Gottesman-Knill Theorem

The Gottesman-Knill theorem states that any quantum computation that involves only Clifford gates, preparation of stabilizer states, and measurements in the computational basis can be efficiently simulated on a classical computer.

Quantum Error Correction

Clifford circuits are fundamental in the implementation of quantum error correction codes, such as the Shor code and the Steane code, which protect quantum information from errors.

Quantum Teleportation

Quantum teleportation is a protocol that uses Clifford circuits to transmit quantum information from one location to another, using entanglement and classical communication.

Bell States

Bell states are specific examples of stabilizer states that are maximally entangled. They are used in various quantum information protocols, including quantum teleportation and superdense coding.

Measurement-Based Quantum Computation

Measurement-based quantum computation (MBQC) is a model of quantum computation that uses Clifford circuits to prepare a highly entangled resource state, followed by adaptive measurements.

Surface Codes

Surface codes are a type of quantum error-correcting code that uses a 2D lattice of qubits. Clifford circuits are used to perform error correction and logical operations on the encoded qubits.

Applications

Quantum Clifford circuits have applications in quantum error correction, quantum cryptography, and the efficient simulation of certain quantum systems on classical computers.
